Welcome!


I'm a fourth year Computer Science student at Wellesley College. Some other institutions I've been fortunate enough to study computer science and cognition at include MIT and Oxford University.

My overarching research interests lie in understanding how our brains perceive and reason about the world and using that information to build more intelligent computer systems.

I'm currently on a gap semester from Wellesley and am investigating automated methods of structuring surrogate posteriors for variational inference as a Student Researcher on Google Research's Tensorflow Probability team.

Earlier this year, I worked as a undergraduate research assistant in MIT's Computer Aided Programming Group (run by Prof. Armando Solar-Lezama) under Dr. Zenna Tavares on causal probabilistic modeling. Prior to that, I worked as an undergraduate research assistant at MIT's Computational Cognitive Science Group (run by Prof. Josh Tenenbaum) doing research on efficient inverse graphics for object recognition.

I've also been fortunate to have the chance to work in industry research over my summers.
This summer, I worked on Google Research's Tensorflow Probability team on automatic structured variational inference. I spent last summer training BERT models to generate questions from passages in Google AI Research's Structured Data Research Group. The previous summer, I worked on improving Brain Computer Interfaces at Neurable.

Feel free to reach out to me if you'd like to chat!
