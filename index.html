I recently earned my BA in Computer Science from Wellesley College. During undergrad, I was also fortunate enough to spend some time studying computer science and cognition at MIT and Oxford.

My overarching research interests lie in understanding how our brains perceive and reason about the world and using that information to build more intelligent computer systems. In particular, I'm currently interested in reasoning about causality and uncertainty.

I'm currently interning at Microsoft Research and working with Adith Swaminathan and Harm Van Seijen. In summer 2020, I interned on the Tensorflow Probability Team at Google Research and worked with Emily Fertig and Dave Moore on automatic structured variational inference. In summer 2019, I interned on the Structured Data Research Group at Google Research and trained BERT models to generate questions from passages. I spent the summer before that working on Brain Computer Interfaces at Neurable

During undergrad, I was fortunate to work as an undergraduate research assistant at MIT with Zenna Tavares and Armando Solar-Lezama on causal probabilistic modeling and with Josh Tenenbaum on efficient inverse graphics for object recognition.
