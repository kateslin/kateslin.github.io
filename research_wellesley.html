Guide to Starting Research for the First Time (Wellesley Specific)

I often get asked a lot of questions from first-years and sophomores on how to get involved with research for the first time. Most students also express concern that they feel imperfectly prepared to tackle research and that they wouldn't even know how to begin getting involved.

To the first point, I say: go for it anyways! Everyone is learning as they go. As for the second point, to help reduce some of the barriers of entry, I've put together a guide on how to get involved in research for the first time as a Wellesley student. It covers questions like "How do I figure out what I want to do research on?" and "How is getting involved in research at Wellesley vs MIT vs Harvard different?" and "What should I even say when I reach out to professors?". While this guide does contain some advice specific to Wellesley's Neuroscience Department, a large portion of it is also generalizable to other STEM fields like CS.

If you'd like to contribute to this guide, please reach out to me! Alternatively, if you have more questions, feel free to reach out (especially if you are from a historically underrepresented group!), and I will do my best to get back to you.
