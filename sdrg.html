Google AI's Structured Data Research Group

This summer I had the really exciting opportunity to intern in the Structured Data Research Group at Google AI Research. The Structured Data Research Group does a lot of work with computational journalism, and some past work they've been involved in includes Fact Checking . As an undergrad, there are very few opportunities to get involved in research at the industry level, so I'm very grateful to my hosts Adam Lelkes and Vinh Tran.

While there, my main project focused on utilizing machine learning, natural language processing, and information retrieval techniques to increase news engagement and literacy. During the summer, I also had the opportunity to speak about my research at Google's We Are GWE (Google Women Engineers) conference.



One thing that was really great about Google was how nice and encouraging my hosts were. They were always supportive of me trying new things, and I got the opportunity to explore using state-of-the-art technologies like BERT. Along with another intern in my group, we developed a reading comprehension BERT model to generate interesting questions given textual information, trained on SQuAD (Stanford Question Answering Dataset).

I'm very grateful for this exciting summer of many firsts - first time doing industry research, first time speaking at a tech conference, and first time working on NLP. As the school year approaches, I'm excited to continue to grow and learn new things!
